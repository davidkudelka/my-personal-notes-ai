    * **Chapter 1 - Finite and Infinite Games** 
      * Describing difference between finite and infinite games and that there is infinite game all around us, in almost every aspect of our lifes
      * Finite mindset - focusing on being best, beating competition, but no such things really exist in infinite games
      * **The Infinite Game of Business**
        * Business is great example of infinite game, there is no such thing as winning the business, only thing we can do is to build organization which can be in the game as long as possible.
        * How we can recognize infinite game ? - There is no agree-upon rules we agreed on in business (in footvball there are), so there is no way we can determine who is winning and who not
      * **A tale of Two Players**
        * Describing difference between mindsets in Microsoft and Apple, Microsoft is trying to beat Apple, but Apple is trying to innovate and be better version of themselfs
      * **The Benefits of an Infinite Mindset**
        * If you are playing finite game, there is winner but as well a loser
        * What's best for me - finite mindset
        * What's best for us - infinite mindset
        * The Infinite mindset helps us embrace failures, not be scared of changes. But it's not about that you can weather change but more about being transformed by it
      * **The Detriments of a Finite Mindset in an Infinite Game**
        * If you focus on finite goals in infinite games, you will end up making bad decisions and in the end you won't be able to play a game anymore
      * **The Quagmire of Vietnam in Business**
        * Sometimes you stop innovating and you just responding to competition, you feel like you have to do the same
        * If you focus on the finite goals you most of the time prioritise urgent before the important
        * Finite mindset in a company might lead to focusing on short term goals -> employee layoffs, stop of innovation -> people are scared of losing their jobs, scared of bringing new ideas, they are just trying to do the things the old ways so they won't mess up anything
        * When we play with a finite mindset in the Infinite Game, we will continue to make decisions that sabotage our own ambitions
      * **Lead with an Infinite Mindset**
        * 1. We don't get to choose whether a particular game is finite or infinite
        * 2. We do get to choose whether or not we want to join the game
        * 3. Should we choose to join the game, we can choose whether we want to play with a finite mindset or an infinite mindset
        * Any leader who want to adopt an infinite mindset must follow 5 essential practices
          * 1. Advance aa Just Cause
          * 2. Build Trusting Teams
          * 3. Study your Worthy Rivals
          * 4. Prepare forr Existential Flexibility
          * 5. Demonstrate the Courage to Lead
    * **Chapter 2 - Just Cause**
      * It starting with a story of a scientist which had vision of ending all famine in the world, so he collected all kinds of seeds and crops and started experimenting and developing better ones. Even after his death and occupation of city where they stored everything. Scientist kept working while being in danger and starvation. They had Just Cause.
      * **What a Just Cause Is**
        * A just cause is a specific vision of a future state that does not yet exist, a future state so appealing that people are willing to make sacrifices in order to help advance toward that vision
        * Your why is based on your past and is unique for you,just cause is vision of future
        * A just cause must be
          * For something - affirmative and optimistic
          * Inclusive - open to all those who would like to contribute
          * Service oriented - for the primary benefit of others
          * Resilient - able to endure political, technological and cultural change
          * Idealistic - big, bold and ultimately unachievable
        * It's better to write down Just Cause so the meaning won't get lost and can remain for future generations
    * **Chapter 3 - Cause. No Cause.**
      * It's important to recognise if company has truly just cause or some phrase just to attract employees, customers and etc. Because later on it will come up on surface
      * **Moon Shots Are Not a Just Cause**
        * There are moon shots that can be in many ways very similiar to the Just Cause but they are not, the difference is that they are finite. Let's imagine moon landing by USA, it's inspiring, for something, idealistic, service oriented, but still finite goal that ends at one day and there might not be anything else after it
      * **Being a Best is not Just Cause**
        * Successful product doesn't mean successful company. By focusing on being best we are not focusing on serving customers and hence we can miss opportunities to be "better"
      * **Growth Is Not a Just Cause**
        * Focusing on Growth is wrong as well because we can end up with trying to grow no matter what and loose our direction this way
      * **Corporate Social Responsibility is not Just Cause**
        * The thing that company donates money or sponsoring certain things is not Just Cause either if it's not directly supporting Just Cause
    * **Chapter 4 - Keeper of the Cause**
      * There is story about Walmart, where the company became very popular and successful, because of the service focused approach Sam Walton brought, but after new CEO started, he ruined everything, he started focusing on revenue instead of Just Cause. So even he was more skilled executive he was lacking infinite mindset
      * **We Need a New Title**
        * Words matter and because of that term CEO is quite confusing for most of the people. What does executive means ? Better word for it is CVO Cheif Visionary Officer, in this position we are responsible for keeping the vision clear and manage everyone to help advance it.
      * **Next in the Line for the Job**
        * Usually the next in the line for CEO job is CFO or COO, but those roles require completely different mindset, that's why it's usually disaster when this transition happen. CFO and COO is more of an executor with more finite minded job. It's much better when those 2 work together one as an visionary and the second one as an executor
