    * ### Why building roles and responsibilities
      * Clearly outlined responsibilities adds clarity
      * "Divide responsibilities and no one is responsible" - [[W. Edwards Deming]]
        * It is a big mistake to be not clear about who is responsible for what
    * ### Determining Skill Requirements
      * Review long-term requirements, and it is important to communicate them to your team on every occasion
    * ### How to Structure your Team
      * For start ups - it's better to have full-stack engineers
      * For big business - people more specialise
      * Good model is Spotify model
    * ### Develop and track skills
      * Technical
        * you look at the technologies/tools/frameworks your team is using and start conduct sessions/share links and follow up with them on that
      * Communication skills
        * write presentations, meetings, stand ups, walkarounds, interviews, and interpersonal
      * Leadership skills
        * Delegation
        * Communication
        * Succession planning is a process for identifying and developing new leaders who can replace old leaders when they leave
      * What skillset matrix helps you with
        * Performance evaluation and promotion processes
        * Build awareness for me as a manager to correct issues
        * Encourage self-development
        * Show manager care
        * Track progress for salaries and promotions
        * Build relationships between whole hierarchy
        * Motivate employees
        * Handover, if you are leaving your position 
    * ### Motivate Team
      * Setting up a clear process like onboarding, development, etc.
      * Communicate the business requirements and results so they find their contribution
      * Performance evaluation that recognises high-performant and rewards them, recognises the low-performants, and giving you awarness in order to skill them up
      * Invest in them, otherwise, they will ask "why do we stay?"
      * Build different communication channels between team members so they can easily exchange experiences like code reviews, pair programming, after-work activities
