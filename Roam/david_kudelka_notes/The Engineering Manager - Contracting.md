    * Sometimes it can be hard to come up with process when you are starting professional relationship with someone
    * There is list of questions that can potentially help you with that - it is called **contacting**
    * ## List of questions
      * ### What are the areas that you would like support with?
        * Broad question on purpose, don't make suggestion to the person, note down everything he/she is willing to say to you
      * ### How would you like to receive feedback and support from me?
        * To find out what is the person like, find out what he is comfortable with
        * If they can't give clear answer you can try this
          * Let them imagine, for example, that they were in a meeting, proposing something in front of the room on a whiteboard. Would they be more comfortable with you, their manager, calling them out on an inconsistency in front of the whole room, or alternatively would they prefer you to tell them privately face to face afterward? Would they prefer your comments on a document they have shared face to face, or by email? Once you dig deeper, you may find that your direct reports are all very different.
      * ### What could be a challenge of us working together?
        * it can be different, they are not comfortable public speakers, or you have work with different programming languages
      * ### How might we know if the support I’m offering isn’t going well?
        * Aligning on the signs that leads to worse relationship between you
      * ### How confidential is the content of our meetings?
        * How is sharing information from the meetings confidential? Find out by this scenario for example
          * For example, your direct report may say that her colleague’s performance has been getting worse over the quarter. However, how confidential do they consider this information? Would they feel uncomfortable if you went and followed up directly with that information? Would they feel uncomfortable if they mentioned that the feedback came from them?
    * ## Summary
      * We’ve run through an exercise called contracting that can be used to break the ice, increase understanding, and set expectations between you and your direct reports. It’s useful to use when starting a new role, but it’s also useful to revisit it with time since both of your needs will adapt and evolve.
