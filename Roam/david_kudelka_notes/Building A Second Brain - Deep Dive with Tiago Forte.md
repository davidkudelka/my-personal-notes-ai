    * "Productiviy = Doing your life's work as efficiently and effectively as possible" - [[Tiago Forte]] #quote
    * **Second Brain**
      * It is system of knowledge management that lays outside of your brain
      * **Why Second Brain ?**
        * If you read, you might as well to save your highlights into system and then have access to this content. If you interact with content you just heard, more likely it will stay in your head
        * It's about building your knowledge that can last years and years
        * **3 Advantages**
          * Learning aspect of it - we all absorb information
          * Content Creation - you can use it to gather information and form some output out of it
          * Personal organisation - noting all needed stuff about your health, apartment and etc...
      * **Best Tool ?**
        * Tools
          * Evernote - is for librarians, it's like library
          * Roam - is for gardeners, you creating way how to store it and it's about connections and pathways in the garden
          * Notion - is for architects, people in between Evernote and Roam, grand vision, beautiful highly designed thing, where they can build
      * **Slow burns and heavy lifts**
        * There is some situations where you have to do heavy lift, meaning as hard work.
        * Heavy lifts are limited and as life progress you have less and less time for heavy lifts
        * **Slow burn**
          * You can go step by step, little progress every time and you can really do this with Second Brain, because you have this perfect memory from software
      * **Containers vs Streams**
        * We come from world of **containers**
          * Think of life stages - there is age when you learn, then you work and then you just chill and hang out.
        * Todays, there is much more overlaps, and they are breaking up into streams, it is not only that book selling company sells books, but it can become software company (amazon)
        * There are no clear edges
        * It's good to thing about information we consume as stream, so we can always stop and restart. (There is nothing worse than to think that we have to finish every single book, article, course. It's always better just to take it as stream and feel free to say I had enough and go for something else)
      * **Productive Randomness**
        * There is no perfect system, to some degree we need randomness
        * Sometimes you cannot apply rules to everything and some things needs to grow organically (Like relationships, dogs)
      * **Exploring different way how to operate in the world is very important**
      * **The Value of The Building Second Brain Course**
        * It's not really about the information you gonna receive in the course, but it's more about the interaction and direct interaction with author that can bring you much more value
      * **Principles not Prescriptions**
        * There is no clear prescription for you success, it's about very personal approach, you have to work trough the details.
        * There is some **importance of social learning**
      * **Creation over consumption**
        * It vastly accelerates your learning
        * You are putting your reputation on line when you are creating something and publishing, you have to think much more about it, you ask more questions
        * You don't have to always be in learning mode, you can chill out as well, but what matters is that if you are in working mode, it's beneficial to save all your thoughts into some external system
        * Don't take notes on a stuff that you already know, it's useless, take notes on think that surprised you, that are counterintuitive, bizare
      * **Reading fiction**
        * [[Historical fiction]] - it's largely based on facts, and that there is fictional story, they are not fully fictional but they are as close to facts as possible.
      * **Design Thinking**
        * “Design thinking was a philosophy that came out of the Stanford Design School but was championed by IDEO. Design thinking is just the idea that creating something new is a process and that process is iterative – it’s not just that you do your research, you make something and you finish – it’s more that you put it out and you get feedback which feeds back into the loop and you iterate and continue to improve. It's kind of familiar now but it was the first thing to really introduce this thinking into companies because at the time companies were not used to doing that”.
      * **Daily and weekly reviews**
        * Weekly review - it shouldn't be rigid checklist which you do everytime, you should do it more fluid, personalise it and customise it
          * You can have a checklist, but it should be more like a list of possibilities (reminders)
          * It should prepare you to re-prioritise
      * **Task management**
        * Tasks can be managed on personal level, then you have some output and that can be managed in something like notion
        * You can have full database of full database tasks, but you can at the beginning of each day generate your todo list

  * Summary:
    * "Productiviy = Doing your life's work as efficiently and effectively as possible" - [[Tiago Forte]] #quote
    * **Second brain** is management system which you use to input all information you have, then it should be easily accessible
    * **Slow burns and heavy lifts** - it's better to separate your work into small burns, little chunk of work than do long sessions of hard work, heavy lifts
    * **Principles not Prescriptions** - we want to categorise every thing, but what actually works best is to view every thing as stream which you can tap into and tap out as you like (meaning that you don't have to every time finish everything to 100%)
    * **Productive Randomness** - there needs to be some sort of randomness in your system, there is no perfect system that manages everything perfectly
    * **Exploring different way how to operate in the world is very important** - just explore new untraditional ways how to do things is very important
    * **Principles not Prescriptions** - there is no prescription to success or perfection, every one is different and needs his own approach
    * **Creation over consumption** - creating things is always much better than just consuming things
    * **Daily and weekly reviews** - it shouldn't be rigid thing with set list of checkboxes but more of an open discussion with yourself, re-prioritising
