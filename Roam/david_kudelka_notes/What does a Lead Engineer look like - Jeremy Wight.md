  * notes:
    * Technical competences
      * Scope of oversight
        * Can have oversight of 3-5 projects and understand design patterns, code base  and concerns (architecture)
        * Understanding of impact of his decisions and technical decisions
        * Pro-active in shaping the product with product team and is able to have productive conversation
      * Technical Operations
        * they understand how to scale and how to effectively test, track, log, monitor and evaluate the ongoing health of their product
    * People/Project management competences
      * Scope of Oversight
        * How to break down scope for 3 or more engineers
          * work is chunks that can effectively estimated'
          * taking into consideration who will work on those tasks
          * timing of that work in light of cross-team constraints
      * Accuracy of estimations
        * 70-80% of estimations are successful - by understanding the team and ability to create Tasks/Stories
          * functional coding, research. collaboration, fudge time, test writing, personal QA, delivery
    * Team/Department Improvements
      * Continuous Improvements
        * Always analyzing problems and making suggestions to processes
        * Deep and growing understanding of their product
        * They know their team and products success metrics and are applying that understanding to the products that they build
      * Leadership
        * They are effective in leading through meetings, planning, and any processes which are needed to accomplish the goals of the team
      * Communication
        * They effectively communicate with both sides (engineering / stakeholders)
  * Outcomes:
    * How to effectively refine tasks, break down stories?
    * How to implement improvements to the project (time to research and how to sell those)
      * Estimation of tasks, how to take into consideration who is working on the ticket?
      * How to better understand product?
        * What to do when goals from business are not clear?
    * How to manage team properly (how many 1on1's and how much time to spend with other team members so I can still focus on my own work)?
