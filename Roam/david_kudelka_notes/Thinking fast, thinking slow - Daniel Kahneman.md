    * #  Part 1 - Two Systems
      * ## 1 - The Characters of the Story
        * [[System 1]]
          * **System 1** - operates automatically and quickly, with little or no effort and no sense of voluntary control
        * **System 2** - allocates attention to the effortful mental activities that demand it, including complex computations. The operations of System 2 are often associated with the subjective experience agency, choice, and concentration
          * **System 2** requires attention, which we have just limited amount, everyone has awareness of limited capacity
        * Sometimes if we are paying full attention to one activity we can become temporarily blind to others, we can miss the obvious and we are also blind to our blindness (there is just limited capacity of our attention)
        * ### Plot Synopsis
          * **System 1** and **System 2** works in cooperation, often **System 1** generates ideas and then **System 2** overtakes and work with those initial ideas, suggestions ...
        * ### Conflicts
          * Sometimes we struggle to overcome **System 1** initial responses by our **System 2** logical thinking
        * ### Illusions
          * We have visual but as well cognitive illusions, these are extremely hard to overcome, we can teach **System 2**, that these illusions are illusions but we always fell like they are not initially because of the uncontrollable **System 1** 
      * ## 2 - Attention and Effort
        * We have limited amount of working memory in our brain, different tasks takes different amount of this memory.
        * What takes up the most amount of memory is when you have to use Slow Thinking Fast
        * As you are getting more experienced in a task, it requires less attention and less memory, so you are able to focus on different tasks simultaneously
        * If you are busy with certain tasks, all your attention is allocated to this task, you are more likely to miss obvious things
      * ## 3 - The Lazy Controller
        * Self control requires mental effort so we can say that self-control is tiring
        * The best way how to maintain mental effort for long time is to be in state of **flow**, where people can maintain mental effort for long time without spending mental energy on self control
        * After using self-control we tent to rely much more on **System 1** right after it, because we exhausted all our mental energy for **System 2** self control decision
          * Having enough glucose in the brain plays most likely some role as well, having more brain energy in form of glucose fuels hard thinking of **System 2**, so hungry and thirsty people tend to rely more on **System 1**, because they do not have enough glucose for **System 2**
        * Even intelligent people who can solve very hard problems, sometimes do not invest the hard energy of **System 2** to correct intuitive answers of **System 1**, so we have another thing that can determine persons intelligence and that is awareness and being present (alertness, being more **rational**)
      * ## 4 - The Associative Machine
        *  Big influence on our association capabilities has things called **priming**.
          * If we see, hear or interact with something it tends to influence our initial decision triggered by **System 1**. If we see picture of money on the screen, we tend to be more individualist, help less other people.
          * These changes in our behaviour happens totally without our consciousness and automatically
          * We have to admin and understands those flaws of our mind to be able to control them and prevent them
      * ## 5 - Cognitive Ease
        * If we interact with something that invokes cognitive ease in us, we are more likely to believe it, because it invokes a sense of familiarity, we already encountered this situation, that's why we more likely going to rely on **System 1** cognitive feeling without ever starting **System 2**
          * But if there is something when we are receiving the information that feels like a strain, something unfamiliar, something weird, we are more likely going to use **System 2**, because of this (just because there is something weird)
          * Cognitive ease is associated with good feelings, this has evolutionary reasons, because of this, new things seems as something that evoke cognitive strain and we should approach them more mindfully with **System 2**. Something that we experienced many times before should evoke good emotions and cognitive ease
        * Good emotions supports cognitive ease and sharpens **System 1** and it's capabilities, on the other hand it weakens **System 2**. This works other way around as well, when we are feeling down, it is almost impossible to use good intuition of **System 1**
      * ## 6 - Norms, Surprises and Causes
        * ### Assessing Normality
          *  Our **System 1** is always scanning world around us for abnormalities
          * We can communicate effectively, because we share some norms for categories, items and etc...
          * If we experience something abnormal multiple times, it starts to be less surprising under the circumstances
          * **Example:**
            * How many animals of each kind did Moses take into the Arch?
            * Answer is 0, because it was Noah and not Moses, but because Moses seems probably in this context, our **System 1** doesn't not detect any abnormality at the first glance
        * ### Seeing Causes and Intentions
          * Since young age we tend to look for causes and intentions in the objects, events and their interaction without actually making reasoning why this is happening
      * ## 7 - A Machine For Jumping To Conclusion
        * **System 1** tend to believe everything and it is work of **System 2** to un-believe statements
        * ### Halo Effect
          * First impression we have about situation or person greatly influence how we view the person in the future. If there are positive signs first then we tend to not view the negative subsequent sings so negatively. This works vice versa as well. If there is something negative about the person and then we see something positive about him, we tend to degrade this positive traits
          * What we experience first, influence greatly what we think about the situation or person, all the information we get after the first ones are influenced by the first impressions
        * ### What You See Is All There Is (WYSIATI)
          * Often if we hear one story about situation that happened if often tend to believe that even thought we don't necessarily have enough data or good quality of the data (What we see is all there is).
          * There is list of biases of judgement
            * **Overconfidence** - The confidence that individuals have in their beliefs depends mostly on the quality of the story they can tell about what they seem even if they see little
            * **Framing Effects** - Different ways of presenting same information matters, 90% fat free sounds better than 10% fat
            * **Base-rate neglect** - if we are asked who more fits to be librarian and then we are given personality traits, we often pick the one that sounds the best to us, without considering other possibilities 
      * ## 8 - How Judgments Happen
        * ### Basic Assessments
          * We are good at assessing average, but not **sum-like** numbers (we can tell if two lines are same length or what is average length of multiple lines, what we struggle with is to tell sum length of all lines)
          * If we don't have any information about stranger we tend to judge his intention based on what we see, facial expression, strength, scars...
          * Evaluating people as attractive or not as a basic assessment. You do that automatically whether or not you want to, and it influences you
          * There are circuits in the brain that evaluate dominance from the shape of the face. He looks the part for a leadership role
        * ### Intensity Matching
          * It's very easy for **System 1** to match things based on intensity
          * For example how tall is the man that is tall as murder is severe crime - pretty tall, not super tall but very tall
          * This is not accurate at all from statistician point of view, but perfectly natural for **System 1** and even for most people for **System 2**
          * The punishment won't feel just unless its intensity matches the crime. Just like you can match the loudness of a sound to the brightness of a light
        * ### Mental Shotgun
          * Our **System 1** tend to scan automatically what we see, shape of the objects, their identity and etc..
          * But we can as well scan something with our **System 1** intentionally, but as we do our **System 1** tend to scan much more information than we actually need
          * Example - Tell me if combination of 2 words rhyme
            * VOTE - NOTE
            * VOTE - GOAT
            * VOTE - NOTE we are able to assess much quicker, but let's look at different example
              * Tell me if the statements are true
                * Some roads are snakes
                * Some jobs are snakes
                * Some jobs are jails
                * All of them are false, but the second one is much easier to tell, because the words doesn't make sense together
      * ## 9 - Answering An Easier Question
        * ### Substituting Questions
          * Instead of answering to hard question we tend to answer to easier question that is derived from the hard question by **System 1**
            * This happens because of Mental Shotgun of our **System 1**
            * Example question - How much would you contribute to save endangered species?
              * What might happen is that our **System 1**, will ask How much emotion do I feel when I think about dying dolphins?
              * When we get answer we need to convert it to dollars to answer original question, and there is example of intensity matching, we take how much anger we feel when dolphins are dying and convert it to dollars and BANG we have answer
        * ### The Mood Heuristic for Happiness
          * There was study done on students
            * How happy are you?
            * How many dates did you have last month?
          * If they asked students in this order there wasn't any correlation between how happy they are and how many dates they had
          * But if we switch the questions, there was clear correlation between those answers. Why? Because **System 1** already has answer to the happiness question if it answered the question about dates, there is clear substitution example.
          * The same can be applied if there will be first question about personal finances
        * ### The Affect Heuristic
          * If we believe in something we tend to thing it's costs are low and it's benefits are high
          * This can be changed by **System 2**, if we learn that something is less risky than we thought 
    * # Part 2 - Heuristics and Biases
      * ## 10 - The Law of Small Numbers
        * We have poor initial understanding of sampling effects
          * Lower number of items have bigger probability to have extreme results than higher number of those items
        * We are pattern seekers, believers in coherent world, in which regularities as pattern of six girls born in a row is not something natural but result of someones planning
        * We pay more attention to the content of the message than to information about their reliability
      * ## 11 - Anchors
        * If we saw, hear some kind of number before we are asked another question this number affects our decision, even if we are thinking that it doesn't
        * There are 2 types of anchoring
          * ### Anchoring as Adjustment
            * We hear the number and out **System 2**, goes down or up from this number that far as we are comfortable
          * ### Anchoring as Priming Effect
            * We hear the number and this number immediately sparks in **System 1** chain of suggestions which influences our decision as well
        * The best way how to fight against anchoring is either way not continue with the negotiation or try to find lowest possible point the can agree on. Doesn't make sense to set another extreme low price anchor

        * This happens in work as well when working in teams, you should always remember that if you feel like
