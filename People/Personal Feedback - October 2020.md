      * Message
        * Clearly identify blockers (that improved significantly recently). Mention people who are blocking you with tags in public channels and if they are online and keep ignoring the message - DM them - I noticed that a lot of people, especially PPMs, have hundreds of messages unread and it might take quite a lot of time until they catch up. DM usually gets read first.
        * Also, always make sure that there is a small backlog of a couple of clearly defined tasks for the day, so that if you are blocked you could switch to one of them. Of course that’s a job of PPM / LE to make sure you have stuff to work on, but it is one thing to easily miss and can be challenging to reshuffle during the day, so your help on this would be much appreciated.
        * When you are overtaking a task after someone or starting a new story - spend 10-20 minutes and hop on a call with people related. Those can be engineers who worked on it in the past, PPM, designer, engineer who is supporting other related parts of the product - the point is, someone already thought about this feature / piece of code before and can give more context. In the ideal world stories in Jira and docs in Confluence describe the task with 100% of details, but I haven’t seen it a single time in startup environment - everything is either very new or changes very fast, and even though we are doing our best to keep stuff actual, most of the time it is not the case.
        * Don’t be defensive, explain to other people what you are doing calmly. That’s the issue that I see for majority of engineers and that can easily spoil the relationships in the team. At the same time if you feel like being under too much pressure, please reach out to me / Martina / Karol.
        * At the start of the task, get clear understanding of the expectations regarding the time and effort spent for it. Outline for yourself a general plan and come up with your own estimate - if it is higher than what is expected by person providing a task - sync with them, it might be a case that either task is not clear, you don’t see the optimal way or the person passing a task doesn’t see the complexity properly - in any case you will end up with the same understanding and organize stuff better. If you see that your initial estimate is getting inaccurate for some reason (e.g. delays or blockers), reach out to the team right away to be able to plan about it.
