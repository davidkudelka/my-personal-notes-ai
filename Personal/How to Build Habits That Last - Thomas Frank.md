  * Notes:
    * **1. Section - Introduction**
      * We can define what goals are important to me, breaking them down into something actionable and then learning to sticking with those habits
    * **2. Section - Perfect is the Enemy of Good**
      * It is ok that some things take time. If we give ourself permission that certain things gonna take time, we more likely gonna stick to our routines.
      * PERFECT IS THE ENEMY OF GOOD
      * It's good to have sustainable pace. Slow growth.
      * Doing things gradually is the key to learning and growing
    * **3. Section - Determining Your Goals**
      * The Impossible List
        * List of your goals
        * Easy to access and you see it often
        * Goals can be anything, you can list everything to piece of paper, but before you put it on actual list you have to ask yourself a question and that is **Why does this goal matter to you?** ( it needs to give you motivation in long run )
        * **Which goals should you focus on? (and how many)**
          * Commit to 3-4, depends how hard and ambitions they are
          * He mentions a story with Warren Buffet, where pilot of his private jet is asking him how to achieve goals in his life ? So he makes him write on paper 25 goals he wants to achieve and then pick just 5 of them. After you pick 5, you have to thrash all other ones
            * **The Dip** - Pick just few goals is very important because of effect that's called The Dip and it means that some things are very entertaining when we are not going too deep, so every time somethings get difficult, we just switch to something else so it's entertaining again
          * **3 types of progress**
            * Gear Acquisition
              * A lot of satisfaction
              * It's beginning
            * Learning
              * There is still lot of new stuff, still quite satisfaying
            * Deliberate practice, aka work
              * This one brings the most rewards, but it's the toughest
          * **What if I can't achieve all my goals?**
            * Nobody has time to complete everything
            * You can focus on limited number of goals
    * **4. Section - Setting Yourself Up For Success**
      * Make your goal specific
      * Make it actionable
      * **What's the first step?** (ask yourself)
      * **Our Environment**
        * 20 Second Rule - make things you want to do as easy as possible and things you want to get rid of as hard as possible
      * **Summary**
        * What is your goal ?
        * What is the daily amount of practice you can put into your goal ?
        * How can I make it easier to do that ?
    * **5. Section - Using External Systems**
      * It's good to make yourself accountable about your goals, you can use some systems, the important is to see the progress visually 
      * "Inspiration is for amateurs. The Rest of us just show up and get to work" #quote - Chuck Close
    * **6. Section - External Systems: The Hard Way**
      * Book mention - [[The Motivation Hacker - Nick Winter]] #book-mention
    * **7. Section - Anticipating Pain Points**
      * Commit to smaller goals and let yourself push past them
      * Find ways how to automate stuff, so next time it's easier to get started
      * Don't be too hard on yourself
      * Exercise - **List 3-5 pain points you anticipate coming up, then brainstorm ways to mitigate or solve them**
    * **8. Section - Tracking, Reviewing and Reflection**
      * [[App Haibitica]] - for tracking your habits
      * It's good to make 14 days cycles and track your habits during this period, after this make small review, list all your reasons why you failed or all your success and overall feedback
    * **9. Section - What to Do When You Fail**
      * If you fail, you might find helpful spiraling back up, slowly starting again with your habit, instead of going super hard again
      * Failure is inevitable, but don't let one failure to stop you
    * **10. Section - Final Thoughts**

  * **Summary**
    * 1. It's better to have sustainable growth than overwhelm yourself at the beginning and quit (Things you produce doesn't have to be perfect)
    * 2. All your goals needs to have some reason, so they can motivate you later on
    * 3. It's much better to focus on just few goals, that way you are more likely to stick with them
    * 4.Your environment and feeling of gradual progress matters
    * 5. Use some system to track your habits and something that can motivate you, for example making the goals public
    * 6. Review your progress, evolve
